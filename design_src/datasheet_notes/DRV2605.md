# DRV2605 (Haptic Driver)

Source: `design_src/datasheet/drv2605.pdf`

## Interface
- I2C control interface.
- Supports single-byte and multi-byte register R/W, including sequential addressing.

## I2C Address
- 7-bit device address: `0x5A` (binary `1011010`)
- 8-bit address bytes: write `0xB4`, read `0xB5`

## Key Pins (per datasheet pin list)
- `EN`: device enable input.
- `IN/TRIG`: multi-mode input (I2C selectable as PWM, analog, or trigger).
- `SDA`, `SCL`: I2C.
- `VDD`, `GND`
- `OUT+`, `OUT-`: motor drive outputs.

## Firmware-Relevant Notes For This PCB
- `EN` and `IN/TRIG` are each routed to ESP32 GPIOs.
- `SCL/SDA` are routed to ESP32 GPIOs (separate I2C bus vs shared bus depends on your pin mux choices).

## Digital Polarity (from datasheet)
- `EN` gates active operation:
  - `EN = HIGH`: device active
  - `EN = LOW`: device enters shutdown (lowest power state); registers are not reset

## `IN/TRIG` Semantics (what HIGH/LOW means depends on MODE)
`IN/TRIG` is multi-mode; the meaning of “high/low” depends on the mode configured over I2C.

- External trigger (edge) mode:
  - **Low → High transition** on `IN/TRIG` sets the internal `GO` bit (starts the selected waveform sequence).
  - High → Low transition does nothing (so a short pulse is sufficient).
- External trigger (level) mode:
  - `GO` mirrors `IN/TRIG`:
    - `IN/TRIG = HIGH` ⇒ `GO = 1` (play/continue)
    - `IN/TRIG = LOW` ⇒ `GO = 0` (cancels/stops)
  - Firmware must hold `IN/TRIG` high for the whole waveform if you want it to complete.

## Logic-Level Thresholds (useful when interfacing to ESP32)
- Digital input low (max): `VIL` ≈ `0.5V` (EN, IN/TRIG, SDA, SCL)
- Digital input high (min): `VIH` ≈ `1.3V` (EN, IN/TRIG, SDA, SCL)
